"WELCOME TO MY FAVOURITE DESTINATION IN THE WORLD"

Venice,the capital of northen Italy's Veneto region, is built on more than 100 small islands in a lagoon in the Adriatic Sea. It has no roads, just canals - including the Grand Canal thoroughfare-lined with Renaissance and Gothic palaces. The central square, Piazz San Marco, contains St. Mark's Basilica, which is tiled width Byzantine mosaics, and the Camanile bell tower offering views of the city's red roofs.

5 GREAT PLACES TO VISIT IN VENICE

  1. BASICICA DI SAN MARCO

    Basilica di San Marco is the most important church in the city and one of the most important monuments in italy, fot its opulent design, gold ground mosaics, and its status as a sumbol of Venetian wealth and power, from the 11 th century on the building has been known by the nickname Chiesa d'Oro(Church of gold).

  2. PALAZZO DUCALE

    Palzzo Ducale is a masterpiece of Gothic art and repository of innumerable art treasures, it features a number of different and intricate architectural details, dating from the 9th century.

  3. PONTE DEI SOSPIRI

    Ponte dei Sospiri is a baroque bridge that once connected Palazzo Ducale to the prisons. The bridge of sighs is one Venice's most important sights.

  4. CANAL GRANDE

    Canal Grande is known as the most beautiful street in the world. The Canal Grande is the main waterway of Venice lined in both sides by an uninterrupted series of palaces and materpieces.

  5. TEATRO LA FENICE

    Teatro La Fenice was twice destroyed by fire and twice restored to its former spledour. For centuries La Fenice has been Venice's principal stage for world-class opers, music, theater and ballet.
